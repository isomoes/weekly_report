# Blog Guide

Use this guide for technical blog posts, blog intros, and article rewrites.

## Goal

Write technical content that is readable and engaging while staying precise and faithful to the underlying work.

## Style

- Use a conversational but professional tone.
- Keep sentences and paragraphs digestible.
- Add smooth transitions between sections and ideas.
- Convert rough bullets into readable prose, except when tutorials or stepwise instructions are genuinely clearer.

## Formatting

- Use emphasis strategically with Markdown when it helps scanning.
- Use reference links with descriptive anchor text when links are part of the task.
- Add introductions and conclusions when the draft needs framing.
- Use emojis only if the user's context already suggests that tone; do not force them.

## Technical Integrity

- Keep terminology accurate and consistent.
- State facts and measurements instead of hype.
- Present outcomes in plain language without overselling them.
- Preserve important implementation details when they help the reader understand why the work matters.

## Mermaid Guidance

Use a Mermaid diagram only when it improves clarity, such as for:

- process flow
- comparison of options
- system relationships
- architecture overview

Keep diagrams simple, readable, and aligned with the surrounding explanation. Do not force a diagram into every post.

## Blog Quality Bar

Aim for writing that:

- opens with a clear hook or framing sentence
- explains why the work matters to the reader
- keeps technical depth without sounding like an internal status report
- ends sections cleanly and transitions naturally to the next idea

## Good Direction

Prefer this kind of opening:

"This week I refactored the training pipeline around a modular loader, which made it easier to isolate data-path problems and created a cleaner setup for benchmarking batching strategies."

Avoid this kind of opening:

"The system was improved in various ways and now works much better than before."
